What this is

A rulebook, not a moodboard.

Third Space lives on Instagram first. The job of this kit is to keep every post recognisable as Third Space — whether you, a designer, or an AI tool makes it. If it's in here, it's a rule. If it's not, it's still being decided.

Brand belief · in her voice

What I keep coming back to.

You see women holding all of it. The family. The work. The everything. But you never really hear them talk about the third version of themselves underneath — the one with her own ambition, taste, doubt, and want.
I don't think we're choosing between those three. We're learning to hold all of them, edit what no longer fits, and build a life that's a bit more our own.
There's no having it all. Trying to is the trap. Every woman I know is making hard trade-offs, just quietly. The job here is to say them out loud and stop pretending the impossible benchmark is real.

Every visual choice in this kit ladders up to one of these:

A woman is more than the roles she's in.

Never reduce her to mum, founder, or wife alone. Visual cues never code to "this is a mum brand."

Ambition doesn't disappear. It changes shape.

Show the deliberateness behind any move, not the move itself. Language is specific, not aspirational.

Motherhood can grow who you are. It doesn't have to erase her.

Portraits never reduce her to the mother role — composition shows the room, the work, the trade-off, not the child.

Work, family, money, friendships, what you actually want — all part of one life.

No category badges, no lifestyle silos, no pillar tags. The feed reads as one woman holding it all, not five separate verticals.

Source: conversational brand belief in NORTH-STAR.md. The formal version is preserved in SOURCE-OF-TRUTH.md. If they ever conflict, North Star wins — it's Bon's voice. Pillar guardrail: three pillars stay broad (ambition · identity · motherhood). Never code to one — that's the explicit rule in the project CLAUDE.md.
What the name means

Third Space says two things at once.

The metaphor matters because it tells anyone working on this brand what they're trying to make space for. From NORTH-STAR.md.

01

First space

Family. The one that pulls hardest.

02

Second space

Work. The one that defines you from the outside.

03

Third space

The one I keep losing. The part of me that isn't either.

And: it says you don't have to choose. You're allowed your own identity, separate from being a mum or a worker. Not at the expense of either. Alongside both.
When you're stuck

Three checks before you publish.

If a post fails any of these, kill it. From the "when I'm lost on what to make" section of NORTH-STAR.md.

  1. Does the story make her think "I thought I was the only one"?
    This is the floor. Recognition is the job. Without this, nothing else matters.
  2. Does it show one real choice this woman made?
    Not advice. A door she didn't know was there. Without this, it's a mirror with no window.
  3. Is the woman someone who didn't disappear into the roles?
    If she did, she's the wrong subject. The whole point is finding women who kept themselves.
The negative tests, also: if it could appear on any working-mom platform, not Third Space. If you'd feature her just because her story is nice, not Third Space. If the post leaves the reader feeling she should be doing more, not Third Space.
Foundations at a glance

The fixed parts of the system.

Borrowed from Spotify's social style guide: a brand has mandatory elements (don't break) and flexible elements (room to play). Here's what's mandatory for Third Space.

Mandatory

Two fonts.

Fraunces + Karla. Nothing else. See type →

Mandatory

Five colours.

Cream, Ink, Terracotta, Plum, Mustard. No bright colour, no pure white. See palette →

Mandatory

Five post types.

Cover, Pull quote, Portrait, Atmospheric, Editor's note. See posts →

Mandatory

One imagery rule.

Colour goes on type. B&W goes on imagery. See photography →

Decisions log: every meaningful call this kit makes — fonts, photography treatment, wordmark direction, pillar signposting, palette — is logged with reasoning at /decisions. Nine decisions locked in v2 (D01–D09). Don't undo a locked decision without writing a new entry that explains why.
