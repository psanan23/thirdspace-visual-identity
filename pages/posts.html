Anatomy

What's on each one.

1 · Cover

The first post when we publish a new story.

  • · Background: Bone
  • · Headline centered — Fraunces Light, ~32–40px on a phone
  • · Her first name + one identifying detail underneath — italic Fraunces, Tobacco
  • · No quotation marks (the headline isn't a quote, it's a hook)
  • · No pillar tag. Content does the categorising; tracking pillars happens internally.
2 · Pull quote

The post that gets screenshotted.

  • · Background: Bone (full bleed)
  • · One sentence — italic Fraunces, Ink, 12–22 words
  • · Word-for-word from the woman. We don't paraphrase.
  • · Attribution underneath — Karla, Tobacco, small caps optional
  • · Quotation marks in.
3 · Portrait

A photo. No front-facing face.

  • · From behind, from the side, just her hands, the room she's in
  • · Photo fills the post — no padding, no border
  • · Small caption bottom-left in Bone (cream) text
  • · Nothing else on the post
  • · Front-facing face is allowed only if she explicitly consented
4 · Atmospheric

A breathing-room post.

  • · Still life, art, window light, a cup of coffee on a notebook
  • · Just the photo. No text.
  • · The only post type where stock or AI imagery is allowed
  • · Used to set mood and pace the feed
  • · Roughly every 3–4 posts
5 · Editor's note

The platform speaks. Rarely.

  • · Background: Linen (a slight shift from Bone)
  • · 30–60 words — Fraunces Medium, Ink
  • · Sign-off in italic Fraunces, Tobacco: "— Third Space"
  • · Maximum one in every nine posts
  • · Used to mark milestones, name a season, set a frame

Feed rules

Read every nine posts as one row.

  1. Every nine posts should include all five post types at least once.
  2. Two pull quotes never sit next to each other.
  3. Two portraits never sit next to each other.
  4. Atmospheric posts give the feed air. Use them every three to four posts.
  5. Editor's notes are rare. Maximum one in every nine posts.
  6. The Cover post leads a story. Pull quote and Portrait sit within it. Story Set order: Cover → Quote → Portrait → Quote.
The Story Set

How a single woman's story posts in sequence.

One featured woman = one Story Set. Four cards published in order, one per day. The Reel comes a day later.

  1. Card 1 — Cover
    Who she is + one defining quote as the headline.
  2. Card 2 — Before / After
    How her path looks now vs. how she expected it to look.
  3. Card 3 — The infrastructure question
    Help, money, trade-offs, how she actually makes it work day to day.
  4. Card 4 — The unsaid
    The thing she hasn't said out loud, or what she'd tell her earlier self.
