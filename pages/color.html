Five colours that do real work.

Cream is the room. Ink is the text. Terracotta carries the warmth. Plum carries the depth. Mustard is the rare moment of joy.

The palette

What each colour is for.

Cream and Ink hold most of the system. Terracotta does the editorial lifting on covers, callouts, and sigs. Plum handles the deep moments — founder quotes, Editor's note rules, dark backgrounds. Mustard appears rarely, only on featured-story moments. Tobacco stays as a utility brown for attribution and small caps.

Colour rules · locked

Don't break these.

  1. Two out of every three posts use Cream as the background.
  2. Terracotta is the primary feature. Plum is the depth. Use one or the other on a given post, not both.
  3. Mustard is for featured-story moments only. Maximum one in every nine posts.
  4. Never use more than three colours on a single post.
  5. No pure white. No pure black. No bright colour outside this palette. Ever.
  6. Colour goes on type. B&W goes on imagery. See the photography page for what this means in production.
