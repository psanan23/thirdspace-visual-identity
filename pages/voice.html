07 — Tone of voice

How we sound, in writing.

Captions, DMs, comments, the bio, every interview intro. Three attributes hold this together. If a draft fails one, redo it.

Three voice attributes

Recognised, specific, honest.

Adapted from the Spotify guide's tone-of-voice frame — three attributes, mutually reinforcing. None is optional. The voice fails when one drops out.

01 — How she feels reading

Recognised

The reader thinks "that's exactly it." Words are doing recognition work — not motivation, not consolation. We name what she's already feeling but hasn't said.

02 — How we write

Specific

Real details over abstraction. Her age, her job, the hour of day, the actual choice. No "this season of life." No "the chapter we're in." Numbers, places, the word she actually used.

03 — How honest

Honest

No tidy resolution. No "and it all worked out." The mess stays in. If a sentence reads as the performance of having arrived, it's wrong — try again.

Caption specs

What goes under every post.

Captions are short. They never editorialise. They get out of the woman's way.

Cover post caption

2–3 sentences. Her age, her one identifying detail, the frame of the story. No "we sat down with…" No "in this story…"

Maya, 38. Founder of a clinic she built after leaving the VP track. Two daughters. This is the version of her life she didn't expect to build, and the parts she's still learning to defend.

Pull quote caption

One sentence of context. Or no caption at all. If the quote needs a paragraph, the quote is the wrong quote.

From Maya's interview, on the guilt she hasn't reframed yet.

Portrait caption

First name + location. That's it.

Maya, Tiong Bahru.

Atmospheric caption

None, usually. If one is needed, a single phrase in italic Fraunces. Never a sentence.

Tuesday, 7:14 a.m.

Voice — write like this

  • "She rebuilt herself completely. And didn't recognise her either."
  • "The guilt of not missing them when she's at work — that one she hasn't reframed yet."
  • "My step back was the most strategic move I made all year."
  • "The friendships that couldn't hold two versions of the same woman."
  • Direct quotes, word-for-word. The woman's own framing.

Voice — not this

  • "In this season of motherhood…"
  • "We sat down with Maya to chat about…"
  • "This is for the mama who…"
  • "You've got this." / "You're not alone." / "We see you."
  • "Slay." / "Era." / "Main character energy."
  • Anything that ends in an exclamation mark.
Community management

DMs, comments, replies.

Reply to a comment

Reply in the woman's register. Short. Specific. Never "thank you 🙏". If someone writes "this is exactly me right now," the reply is: "Which part." Open the door.

DM from a potential subject

Send the manifesto PDF first. Then book a 45-minute call. Don't pitch the platform — let the document do that.

DM from a reader

If she shares her own story unprompted, reply. Ask one specific question back. Don't recruit her on the first message — that's the second message.

Negative or "this isn't for me" comments

Don't engage. Don't delete unless it's abusive. The audience defines itself by who stays.
