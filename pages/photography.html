06 — Photography

iPhone-led, never iPhone-looking.

Every portrait is shot on iPhone Pro Max. That's a constraint, not an excuse. This page tells you how to make it work.

Placeholder · v2 first pass. The full shooting and editing guide (subject framing, light, white balance, presets) is the largest open piece in the kit. This page captures the principles that are already locked. The next iteration of this kit fills in the practical "how to shoot" details.
Principle 01

No front-facing faces. Unless she said yes.

Default to behind, side, hands, the room she's in. Front-facing is the exception, requested in writing, never assumed. This protects the woman and creates a distinctive visual signature for the feed.

Principle 02

The image should not be the best photo of her.

Every featured woman picks three to five images that represent this season of her life. Not headshots. Not "best of." What she values, what she's becoming, the texture of her actual days. These become the basis for her interview, her visual direction, and her Story Set.

The brief to her: "Send me three to five images from your camera roll — not necessarily of yourself — that feel like this season of your life. The window you look out of. What's on your desk. The book you keep returning to. Something you almost deleted but didn't."
Principle 03

The atmospheric post is the only place stock or AI is allowed.

Portraits are real, sourced from her or shot by us. Atmospheric posts (still life, light, art, mood) can be stock or AI. This is the only exception in the kit. Cover, Pull quote, and Editor's note never use stock imagery — they're typographic.

Principle 04 · locked v2

Hybrid treatment. B&W for interviews. Colour for everything else.

Borrowed from Goop's playbook: the interview is the moment that gets the editorial bite, and the visual cue separates it from the rest of the feed without needing a label. If the post is part of an interview Story Set (Cover, Pull quote, Portrait), the portrait is B&W. If it's Atmospheric or part of regular content, the photo holds the warm palette.

Interview portraits — B&W

Every portrait inside a Story Set. Forces specificity in light and gesture. Marks the interview against the warm feed — no label needed.

Atmospheric + everything else — warm muted colour

Still lifes, mood frames, non-interview content. Pulls the palette through. Aesop / Luminary register, applied lightly.

Why the split works: the feed signals "this is an interview" visually, not with a tag. The reader learns the system within three posts. Same logic as Goop's B&W podcast portraits standing apart from their colour lifestyle feed.
Photography rules — locked

What's already decided.

  1. No front-facing faces without explicit consent.
  2. The image is not "the best picture of her." It's a frame she chose.
  3. No white studio backdrops. Ever.
  4. Stock and AI imagery is allowed only on Atmospheric posts.
  5. No heavy edits — no skin smoothing, no portrait mode, no obvious presets.
  6. If you can tell which iPhone preset was used, the edit is wrong.
