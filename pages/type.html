03 — Typography

Two fonts. No third.

Fraunces for everything big. Karla for everything small. Free on Google Fonts. The contrast does the editorial work.

The big text font

Fraunces

Use for post titles, pull quotes, editor's notes, and the wordmark. Serif. Reads like a magazine. Light weight (300) when big. Italic when it's a direct quote.

HeadlineFraunces Light 300
She didn't stop being ambitious. The shape of it changed.
Pull quoteFraunces Italic 400
"My step back was the most strategic move I made all year."
Editor's noteFraunces Medium 500
We started Third Space because no one was telling these stories at this register. The women were always there. The room wasn't.
The small text font

Karla

Use for body text, pillar tags, image captions, attribution lines. Clean sans-serif. Regular (400) for body. Medium (500) for tags, all caps, wider letter spacing.

BodyKarla Regular 400
Maya left her VP role at thirty-six. The cost was the title and sixty percent of the income. The return was three afternoons a week with her daughters and a side practice she now runs on her own terms.
Pillar tagKarla Medium 500 · all caps · 0.28em tracking
Work & Ambition
AttributionKarla Regular 400 · 11px · Tobacco
Maya, founder, two daughters
Type rules

Five rules, no exceptions.

  1. Fraunces goes on top. Karla goes underneath. Never the other way around.
  2. Don't bold body text to add emphasis. If it needs to stand out, make it bigger or italic.
  3. Don't mix italic and roman in the same line.
  4. Maximum two type sizes per post.
  5. No third font. Not for "fun." Not for one campaign. Not ever.
