08 — Briefing AI tools

Copy. Paste. On-brand output.

Ready-to-use prompts for Claude, ChatGPT, and Midjourney. Each one references this kit so the model has everything it needs in one block.

The universal brief

Paste this above any prompt.

Drop this paragraph in front of any creative task — caption, headline, image, post draft. It loads the brand into the model's context in ~150 words.

Paste this
You are writing for Third Space, an Instagram-first editorial platform for the multi-dimensional mother in Southeast Asia — ambitious women navigating career, identity, and motherhood at once. Audience: founders, creatives, professionals in their 30s–40s in Singapore and SEA. Voice: recognised, specific, honest. We name what she already feels but hasn't said. Real details over abstraction. No tidy resolution. Never use: motivational language, "mama," "this season," "we sat down with," exclamation marks, girlboss vocabulary, or wellness-speak. Visual system — Goop Warmth palette: Cream #F4ECDF (background), Ink #1A1816 (text), Terracotta #C8623B (primary feature), Plum #5D3B3F (second-tier depth), Mustard #D6A538 (rare accent · 1 in 9). Fonts: Fraunces (serif, big text) and Karla (sans, small text). Wordmark: Fraunces Medium, all-caps, stacked on two lines (THIRD / SPACE). Five post types: Cover, Pull quote, Portrait, Atmospheric, Editor's note. Critical imagery rule: colour goes on type, B&W goes on imagery. Every photographic post (Portrait and Atmospheric) is black and white. Colour only appears on typographic posts. Reference brands we pull from: Luminary Mothers (editorial), Aesop (palette restraint), Goop (typographic clarity, B&W interview portraits). We do not pull from Gen-Z entertainment brands or wellness platforms.
For Claude / ChatGPT — writing

Caption + headline prompts.

Pull quote selection

Prompt
[Paste universal brief above] Here is a transcript from an interview with [name, age, one identifying detail]: [paste transcript] Pull 5 candidate sentences for a Pull-quote post. Rules: — 12 to 22 words each — Word-for-word from her, no paraphrasing — Each must stand alone with no context — Each must do at least one of: normalise the mess, expand possibility, satisfy curiosity — No motivational tone, no resolution Return them as a numbered list, with a one-line note on which content pillar each belongs to (Identity & Becoming / Work & Ambition / Relationships & Systems / The Unsaid).

Cover headline generation

Prompt
[Paste universal brief above] Subject: [name, age, defining frame in one sentence] Pillar: [01 Identity / 02 Work / 03 Relationships / 04 Unsaid] Write 4 candidate Cover headlines. Rules: — Sentence form, 8 to 14 words — Fraunces Light will set it big, so the rhythm matters — No quotation marks — these are hooks, not quotes — Specific over abstract. Use her real choice, her real number, her real word — Must answer: what does it actually look like when a woman like her holds all of this? Return as a numbered list.

Caption for any post

Prompt
[Paste universal brief above] Post type: [Cover / Pull quote / Portrait / Atmospheric / Editor's note] Subject + frame: [name, age, one-line summary of what the post is about] Write the caption per the rules in voice.html: — Cover: 2–3 sentences, name + identifying detail + frame — Pull quote: 1 sentence of context, or none — Portrait: first name + location only — Atmospheric: a single phrase in italic Fraunces, or nothing — Editor's note: 30–60 words, signed "— Third Space" Never editorialise. Don't say "we sat down with." Don't perform empathy.
For Midjourney / image tools

Atmospheric image prompts.

These are for the Atmospheric post slot only. Portraits are never AI-generated.

Base atmospheric prompt — B&W only

Prompt
[subject — e.g. a half-empty espresso cup on a marble counter, a worn notebook beside it, morning light from a window] Style: black and white, editorial, fine grain. Shot on medium format film, natural window light, shallow depth of field. One clean subject, two-thirds of the frame empty. The mood of Goop's B&W podcast portraits and Loewe Foundation's gallery photography. Not glossy, not heavily produced. No people. No text. Negative: colour of any kind, sepia, warm tone, neon, saturation, HDR, AI-obvious skin, cluttered composition, lifestyle stock cliché, centered subject with everything in focus. Aspect ratio: 1:1 --ar 1:1 --style raw --v 6

What it should NOT look like

Prompt
Reject the output if it has: — Bright or saturated colours — Faces or full figures (Atmospheric posts are object/light-led) — Obvious "stock photo" composition (centered subject, all in focus) — Text overlays, watermarks, brand marks — Glossy magazine retouching — Tropical or beach imagery (Third Space is urban-domestic, not lifestyle-travel) — Anything that reads as wellness, yoga, or self-care content
For Canva / Figma

Briefing a designer or template builder.

If you're handing off to a designer or asking an AI to draft a Canva template, send this.

Build a 1080×1080 px Instagram post template for Third Space. Type: [Cover / Pull quote / Editor's note] Specifications from this toolkit: — Backgrounds available: Cream (#F4ECDF) · Terracotta (#C8623B) · Plum (#5D3B3F) · Ink (#1A1816) · Linen (#EDE3D0) for Editor's note · Mustard (#D6A538) for featured-story moments only. — Fonts: Fraunces (titles, quotes, signoff) + Karla (tags, attribution). No third font. — Cover layout: large headline middle (Fraunces Light 300, 32–40px). First name + identifier at bottom in italic Fraunces, Terracotta on Cream, or Cream on Terracotta/Plum/Ink. No pillar tag. — Pull quote layout: italic Fraunces centered, 12–22 words. Attribution beneath in Karla, Tobacco on Cream backgrounds or Cream on coloured backgrounds. — Editor's note layout: Linen or Plum background. 30–60 words in Fraunces Medium. Signoff in italic Fraunces: "— Third Space". Maximum two type sizes per post. Empty space is required — do not fill the canvas. Two out of three posts use Cream as the background. Mustard appears max once in nine posts.
Test the output

Three-question gut check.

After any AI-generated draft (caption, image, post), run it through these three questions before publishing. If any answer is "no" — redo it, don't ship it.

  1. 01 Would the woman in the audience paragraph stop scrolling for this?
  2. 02 Is it specific enough that no other platform could have made it?
  3. 03 Does it leave the mess in, or did it tidy up?
